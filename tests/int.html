Interview Cheat Sheet

Principal Technical Lead – AI Centre of Enablement

Designed for a Principal‑level interview: focus on judgement, trade‑offs, and operational impact. This is not a content dump — it’s a decision narrative.

Baseline Framing Anchor This

This role exists to turn AI ambition into 3–5 production outcomes, while building the platforms, patterns, and governance so future delivery becomes repeatable — not heroic.
Judgement > Cleverness Trade‑offs > Options Outcomes > Experiments

90‑Second Pitch Opening

  • Already operating in the AI CoE problem space: strategy → delivery → ops
  • Primary strength is decision quality, not just solution design
  • Focus on small, intentional portfolio with clear value and risk ownership
  • Copilot‑first where sufficient; custom agents where differentiated

How You Own the Portfolio Judgement

  • Keep only 3–5 initiatives in flight
  • Each item has: owner, value hypothesis, kill criteria
  • De‑prioritization is a feature, not a failure
  • Roadmap constrains decisions — not a wish list

Delivery Leadership Scrumban

  • Optimize for flow, not ceremonies
  • Limit WIP, surface dependencies early
  • Reality‑based forecasting over optimistic plans
  • Principal focus = system throughput

Applied GenAI Leadership Hands‑On

  • Agentic workflows, RAG, context engineering
  • Design for failure modes & observability
  • Reusable patterns over bespoke builds
  • Go deep only when the decision has enterprise blast radius

Responsible AI by Design Core

  • Governance is shifted left into platforms and defaults
  • Definition‑of‑done includes risk, privacy, and ops
  • Avoid “review at the end” anti‑pattern
  • Speed + safety via embedded controls

Exec Engagement Trust

  • Make trade‑offs explicit
  • Communicate risk, not just progress
  • Decision logs > slideware
  • Comfortable saying “not yet”

Highly Likely Interview Questions + Bullet Answers

  • How do you choose what not to build?
    Use strategy alignment, risk profile, and platform readiness as explicit filters.
  • How do you balance speed and governance?
    Shift governance into patterns and platforms so delivery doesn’t re‑litigate risk.
  • Copilot vs custom agents?
    Copilot as default entry point; custom agents only where value justifies complexity.
  • What makes this role different from a senior tech lead?
    Senior optimizes execution inside the boundary; Principal defines the boundary.

What YOU Should Ask Signals Maturity

  • Where does this role have decision authority vs influence?
  • What is the hardest AI trade‑off you expect this role to help resolve this year?
  • What does success look like at 6 months in concrete outcomes?
Final reminder: Answer in terms of judgement, trade‑offs, and operational consequences. Avoid sounding advisory — speak as someone accountable for outcomes.
