Patch B of the 2026-05-13 Layer 2 audit: add a deterministic scrape_fail pre-check
to `lib/article_filter.layer2_full_body_score` so articles whose scrape returned
a tiny body but the source had real content (body < 500 chars AND content_length
> 2000 chars) are short-circuited to `verdict='scrape_fail'` BEFORE wasting a
DeepSeek LLM call. Today these articles either hit the LLM with truncated bodies
(garbage in / garbage out) or get silently rejected as low-depth — including 7/26
that contain HARD-KEEP keywords (Claude Code, MCP, OpenClaw, CLAUDE.md).

Purpose: stop silently nullifying HARD-KEEP articles via scrape-failure routing;
emit a distinct audit signal ('scrape_fail' verdict) so a future re-scrape job
can find these rows by `WHERE layer2_verdict = 'scrape_fail'`. Patch A (Layer 2
v1 prompt) is deferred until ingest verdict; this is the cheap defensive layer.
